If you are dissatisfied with any aspect of your training, first discuss it with your trainer. If unresolved, a formal complaint can be lodged with DAPA's Consumer Protection Officer. All complaints are handled confidentially and with due process.

In alignment with DAPA's policy on the use of Artificial Intelligence (AI), students with Language, Literacy, and Numeracy (LLND) needs may be supported to use approved AI tools as learning aids. These tools can assist with summarising complex texts, generating study questions, supporting grammar and writing clarity, and brainstorming ideas. When permitted, you must acknowledge how AI was used and demonstrate your own understanding in submitted work. AI should be used to support learning, not to replace student thinking or authentic assessment. DAPA provides guidance on the ethical and responsible use of AI.

Students may defer their training for up to 12 months by completing the appropriate Suspension of Training Form. Fees associated with deferral will be communicated. If the student does not resume their training within the 12-month deferral period, DAPA may cancel their enrolment. Students will be informed if any course content or delivery changes occur during this time.

You can also request a temporary suspension in cases of long-term absence or inability to continue, with similar conditions to deferral.

DAPA offers a range of support services to help you succeed, including Language, Literacy, and Numeracy (LLN) Support and Counselling Services. Study support and study skills programs are also available, with individualized support plans based on students' needs.

You can seek assistance from your trainer, our welfare team, and industry experts within our organisation.

Minimum educational entry level is a School Certificate (Completed year 10), be at least 15 years of age and have basic computer competency. Candidates must have a sound knowledge of English (minimum ESL 5.5), be able to write and read documents in English, a basic concept of maths to Year 10 level and be able to complete an initial skills assessment / LLND check at enrolment. Industry may require students to have recommended vaccinations.

Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) is a process that recognises a student for formal and non-formal training and life/work experience. You will discuss RPL and credit transfer opportunities during the pre-enrolment induction. Evidence that can be used to support an application can include a detailed resume or letters from employers, an interview with the Assessor, work skills/knowledge, attendance to professional development courses, and paid or unpaid work experience. Course recognition will be granted if you provide sufficient evidence of relevant prior learning or experience.

Credit Transfer (CT) is also available for units of competency and modules completed through a different RTO, evidenced by an Academic or USI Transcript.

Assessments are designed to test your knowledge and skills. A range of assessment types are used, including written and oral questions, case studies and scenarios, observation of simulated and real workplace tasks, projects, logbooks, and practical demonstrations. Practical assessments are observed by an assessor in the workplace.

If you are assessed as "Not Yet Satisfactory" for an assessment, you will receive feedback and can reattempt the assessment up to two additional times (three attempts in total).
